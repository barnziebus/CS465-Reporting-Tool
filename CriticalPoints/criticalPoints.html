Critical Points/Sections Calculation

What Are Critical Points/Sections?

A critical section refers to a vulnerable area susceptible to the infiltration of moisture, water, and contaminants that may trigger corrosion within the post-tensioning system. These critical sections encompass regions where voids could potentially develop within ducts or where stress concentration points might form, leading to structural failure.

In compliance with CS 465 guidelines, the identification of critical sections constitutes an integral component of the risk assessment process. These calculations serve as the baseline for determination of the necessary quantity of Duct and Tendon Exposures (DTEs) and/or End Anchorage Exposures (EAEs) needed to inspect a particular structure.

Where a structure has both longitudinal and transverse post-tensioning, the method for calculating the number of critical points has been amended in CS 465, with the two orientations of post-tensioning being addressed separately. The number of critical points and subsequent recommendations for the number of exposures required are calculated by post-tensioning type.

CT = Recommended number of transverse exposures
CPT = Transverse critical points

Determining Critical Points

Typical Locations

  • End anchorages.
  • High points (hoggoing) along the tendon profile (Eg. over intermediate piers).
  • Low points (sagging) along the tendon profile (Eg. midspan).
  • Yeild pont at midspan.
